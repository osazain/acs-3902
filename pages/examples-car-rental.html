Car Rental Database

Overview

What This Example Demonstrates

This example showcases Enhanced Entity-Relationship (EER) modeling concepts including Primary Keys, Foreign Keys, Specialization/Generalization hierarchies, Weak Entities, and Relationships between entities.

🔑 Primary & Foreign Keys

Understanding how entities are uniquely identified and how relationships are established

🔺 Specialization/Generalization

Hierarchical relationships between entity types (Vehicle types)

⚠️ Weak Entities

Entities that depend on another entity for identification (Rental rates)

🔗 Relationships

1:N connections between Customer, Vehicle, Rental Agreement, and Payment

🗄️

Database Schema

Customer

  • PKCust_id
  • Relationship: "is renter" via rental agreements

Vehicle

  • PKVIN
  • Attributes: rental class, model, rental rates
  • {rental class} is a weak entity indicator
  • Specialization categories:
    • Motorcycle
    • Moving van
    • Passenger car
  • Additional attributes: engine size, sq m capacity, passenger seating capacity

Rental Agreement

  • PKagreement #
  • FKVIN (Vehicle)
  • FKCust_id (Customer)
  • Relationship: "is rented via"
  • Payment information linked via agreement #

Payment

  • PK(agreement #, payment type) — Composite Primary Key
  • FKagreement #
  • Attributes: payment date, payment method, amount
🔗

Relationships

Customer → Rental Agreement (1:N) One customer can have many rental agreements
Vehicle → Rental Agreement (1:N) One vehicle can be rented many times (different agreements)
Rental Agreement → Payment (1:N) One rental agreement can have multiple payments
🎓

EERD Concepts Demonstrated

Generalization/Specialization

The Vehicle entity has specializations representing different types of vehicles:

  • Motorcycle — engine size attribute
  • Moving van — sq m capacity attribute
  • Passenger car — passenger seating capacity attribute

Weak Entity

The Rental rates entity depends on {rental class} for identification. This is indicated by the curly braces notation and demonstrates a weak entity that cannot exist without its owning entity.

Composite Primary Key

The Payment entity uses a compound key consisting of (agreement # + payment type). This is necessary because a single rental agreement can have multiple payments of different types (e.g., deposit, full payment, partial payment).
