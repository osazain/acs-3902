Weak vs Strong Entities

Understanding the fundamental difference

🔷 Strong Entity

  • Has its own unique identifier (Primary Key)
  • Can exist independently
  • Does not need attributes from other entities to be identified
  • Example: Customer with CustomerID

🔶 Weak Entity

  • Does not have sufficient attributes to form a unique PK
  • Depends on a parent entity for identification
  • Uses a composite key: Parent PK + Discriminator
  • Example: Order Line Item (needs Order#)
💡 Key Insight: Don't confuse mandatory participation with weakness. A Car Rental Agreement requires a Customer (mandatory participation) but has its own Agreement# — making it strong, not weak!

Why Line Items Are Weak: Data Example

Same line number, different orders

Notice how "Line 1" appears in multiple orders. Without the Order#, we cannot uniquely identify a line item.

Order# PK/FK Line# PART Product Qty Price
101 1 Wireless Mouse 2 $25.00
101 2 USB Cable 1 $10.00
102 1 Keyboard 1 $75.00
102 2 Mouse Pad 1 $15.00
103 1 Monitor 2 $200.00
🔑 Composite Key: The primary key for ORDER_LINE_ITEM is (Order#, Line#). Line# alone is called a discriminator or partial key.
